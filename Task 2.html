Leica M9

The Leica M9 is a full-frame digital rangefinder camera from Leica Camera AG. It was introduced in September 2009. It uses an 18.5-megapixel Kodak image sensor, and is compatible with almost all M mount lenses. Features [ edit] The M9 uses an 18.5-megapixel Kodak (KAF-18500) CCD image sensor that was developed specifically for the camera.

About Us

Leica is a German company that specializes in the manufacturing of high-end cameras, lenses, binoculars, and other optical equipment. The company was founded in 1914 by Ernst Leitz and is headquartered in Wetzlar, Germany. Leica cameras are known for their precision engineering, high-quality optics, and timeless design. They are used by professional photographers and enthusiasts around the world for a wide range of applications, including street photography, photojournalism, and landscape photography.
